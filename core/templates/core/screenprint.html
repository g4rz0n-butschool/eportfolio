Intro

In New Media and Tech, we learn and apply a few technological and design skills. We work in groups to make digital and physical art, while applying theories of social behavior to make experiences that would have an impact.

Project

Photos

To begin the screenprinting project, everyone first started by learning how to use the cameras to their full potential using their manual settings. Settings such as; exposure, aperture, and shutter speed. Once I was happy with my pictures, I imported them into Photoshop.

Photoshop

When everything was imported to Photoshop, Mr. Sinusas taught everyone how to use the software. He taught us how to curve the text around and how to change the colors of the image. That was one of the more fun parts of this whole project. I was able to mess with the layers of the image to make it perfect.

Screenprinting and Final Results

Saving the best for last, here is the screenprinting stage. Everyone needed to export their image, with different layers if they used multiple colors. Then a vinyl cutter was used to cut said images into a piece of vinyl. Then we had to take the cut pieces off of the vinyl and put the rest on a frame. Everyone melted the vinyl and then the actual screenprinting began. I mixed some blue and green with a hint of yellow to make a tealish color. The other color was used with blue and red, which made a dark purple color. I was able to practice on paper, then a piece of cloth, and finally a shirt of my own.
